## Libellulidae

[← Back to Libellulidae in Family Index](/field-notes/odonata/anistoptera/family#libellulidae)
{: style="text-align: center;"}

**Libellulidae** (skimmers) are the largest and most diverse dragonfly family, encompassing many broad-bodied, often colorful species that commonly perch on twigs, rocks, and vegetation. Wing patterns and bold markings are frequent in this family.

### Field marks
- Broad abdomen and robust body
- Wings frequently patterned or marked; held horizontally at rest
- Perching behavior common; varied flight styles

### Habitat
Found at nearly all freshwater habitats—ponds, lakes, marshes, and slow streams—as well as nearby open areas.

### Representative species
- Common Whitetail (*Plathemis lydia*)
- Twelve-spotted Skimmer (*Libellula pulchella*)
- Widow Skimmer (*Libellula luctuosa*)
