## Dragonfly Families

This page provides a practical overview of **dragonfly families** (suborder *Anisoptera*), emphasizing visual characters, flight, and habitat useful for field recognition.

The goal is practical grouping rather than strict taxonomy — use posture, wing venation, eye separation, body shape, and flight style to narrow an observation to a family.

---

## Darners (*Aeshnidae*)

Large, long-bodied dragonflies with powerful, direct flight. Eyes typically meet at the top of the head and they often patrol strongly over water and vegetation.

Field marks:
- Large size and long abdomen
- Eyes broadly contiguous dorsally
- Strong, fast flight; often patrols over open water or along edges

Common examples:
- Common Green Darner
- Shadow Darners

---

## Clubtails (*Gomphidae*)

Medium-sized, often stocky dragonflies distinguished by widely separated eyes and frequently a widened “club” at the abdomen tip in males. Many species perch on the ground or low vegetation.

Field marks:
- Eyes clearly separated on the head
- Often a bulbous terminal abdomen in mature males
- Typically perch on sandy banks or bare ground near streams and rivers

Common examples:
- Common Clubtail
- Lancet Clubtails

---

## Skimmers (*Libellulidae*)

The most diverse and familiar family; broad-bodied and often colorful. Many species perch conspicuously on twigs and rocks, “bobbing” between vantage points.

Field marks:
- Broad abdomen and robust body
- Wings often patterned or marked, frequently held horizontally at rest
- Perching behavior common; varied flight styles

Common examples:
- Common Whitetail
- Twelve-spotted Skimmer
- Widow Skimmer

---

## Emeralds (*Corduliidae*)

Medium-sized dragonflies often with metallic or iridescent green coloration on the thorax and eyes; agile fliers that hunt over water and open areas.

Field marks:
- Metallic green sheen to thorax and eyes
- Fast, erratic flight; often flies over open water
- Slender to moderately robust body

Common examples:
- Baskettails
- Emeralds (various genera)

---

## Spiketails (*Cordulegastridae*)

Stream-associated dragonflies with long, slender abdomens and striking yellow-and-black markings in many species. They are strong fliers and often patrol along stream corridors.

Field marks:
- Long, cylindrical abdomen
- Contrasting yellow and dark patterning
- Strong association with flowing water

Common examples:
- Spiketails (various genera)

---

## Cruisers (*Macromiidae*)

Fast-flying, long-legged dragonflies that often cruise continuously above water. Eyes are large and often touch at the top of the head.

Field marks:
- Continuous cruising flight over water
- Long legs and streamlined body
- Eyes often meeting dorsally

Common examples:
- Cruisers (Macromia spp.)

---

## Petaltails (*Petaluridae*) and Other Families

Large, often relict species associated with bogs and fens (*Petaluridae*), plus occasional regional families. These are less frequently encountered but notable when present.

Field marks:
- Very large size (Petaluridae)
- Habitat-specialist distributions (bogs, fens)

---

## Using This Page

This page is a working reference for organizing observations and building familiarity. Family-level recognition helps prioritize identification features for genus and species-level work: wing venation, abdominal markings, eye separation, and habitat.

As the field notes grow, we can expand individual family pages with species lists, photos, and diagnostic keys.
