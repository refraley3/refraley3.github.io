## Aeshnidae

[← Back to Aeshnidae in Family Index](/field-notes/odonata/anistoptera/family#aeshnidae)
{: style="text-align: center;"}

**Aeshnidae** (darners) are large, long-bodied dragonflies known for powerful, direct flight and eyes that meet broadly on the top of the head. They are frequent patrollers of ponds, lakes, and slow-moving water and are commonly observed in open habitats.

### Field marks
- Large size, long abdomen
- Eyes contiguous dorsally or narrowly separated
- Strong, rapid flight; often patrols fixed routes over water

### Habitat
Often associated with ponds, lakes, and marshy margins; adults patrol open water and nearby vegetation.

### Representative species
- Common Green Darner (*Anax junius*)
- Other Aeshna and Anax species
