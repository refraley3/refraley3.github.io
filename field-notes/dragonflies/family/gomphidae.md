## Gomphidae

[← Back to Gomphidae in Family Index](/field-notes/dragonflies/family#gomphidae)
{: style="text-align: center;"}

**Gomphidae** (clubtails) are typically medium-sized, stocky dragonflies distinguished by widely separated eyes and, in many species, an expanded terminal abdomen (the “club”) in males. They are often found along rivers and streams and frequently perch on sandy banks or bare ground.

### Field marks
- Eyes widely separated on the head
- Frequently a widened terminal abdomen in males
- Often perch on ground, sandy banks, or low vegetation

### Habitat
Common along flowing water—rivers and streams—and adjacent open banks or gravel bars.

### Representative species
- Common Clubtail (*Gomphus* and related genera)
