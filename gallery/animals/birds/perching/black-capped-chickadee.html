Poecile atricapillus

The Black-capped Chickadee (Poecile atricapillus) is a small northern songbird recognized by its black cap and throat, white cheeks, soft gray back, and pale underside. Its compact shape and inquisitive behavior make it a familiar presence in woodland edges, parks, and backyard feeding areas.

For identification details and comparison with similar species, see Poecile atricapillus in the Field Notes section.

E21A2652 leads the page because the eye remains crisp beneath the dark cap. The slightly forward angle brings the bird's expression into view, while the ruffled breast and shoulder feathers give the portrait a restless, wind-tossed character. The curved perch also creates a strong visual line that rises directly toward the chickadee.

Photographer's Perspective

These photographs are less about a polished, still portrait than about the chickadee's character in a passing moment. The wind has lifted and separated the plumage, making the small bird appear both disheveled and completely alert. E21A2652 supplies the stronger expression, while E21A2654 broadens the view into a lively profile.
