Enallagma civile

A slender Familiar Bluet rests among trail-side grasses and nearby wetland vegetation, its narrow body and folded wings blending easily into the green margins of the habitat. These images emphasize both the delicate macro details of the insect and the variation seen among female bluets.

Female bluets can vary noticeably in color and abdominal pattern. Some individuals appear bright blue with strong pale markings, while others look much darker, with reduced blue on the terminal abdominal segments. Close views of the face, thorax, legs, and abdominal banding are especially useful for comparing individuals in the field.

For identification details and comparison with similar species, see Enallagma civile in the Field Notes section.

The same individual reveals the pale thoracic stripes, folded wings, and blue abdominal markings characteristic of female bluets. The insect's small size and slender form make it easy to overlook, but the close-up view highlights the intricate details of its anatomy and coloration.
