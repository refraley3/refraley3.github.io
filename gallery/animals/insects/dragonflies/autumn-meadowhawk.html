Sympetrum vicinum

The Autumn Meadowhawk is a late-season skimmer dragonfly, often seen after many other dragonflies have faded from the field. Mature males are bright red to reddish orange, with clear wings, reddish-brown eyes, dark leg markings, and a compact meadowhawk shape. These photos show individuals perched low in meadow vegetation, using leaves, grass blades, and dry stems as lookout points in warm sun.

This set is not as polished as the Blue Dasher series, but it records a useful late-season meadowhawk encounter. The best image is the dry-stem perch, followed by the close portrait at the leaf edge. The leaf and grass-blade views work well as supporting field-note images because they show the habitat, posture, and low vegetation where these dragonflies can be easy to overlook.

For identification details and comparison with similar meadowhawks, see Sympetrum vicinum in the Field Notes section.

This is the strongest image in the set. The diagonal perch gives the frame a clean structure, while the darker green background separates the dragonfly from the meadow. The subject is small compared with the Blue Dasher portraits, but the pose, warm light, and uncluttered setting make it the best lead photograph for the page.
