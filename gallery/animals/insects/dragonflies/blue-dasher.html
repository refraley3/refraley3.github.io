Pachydiplax longipennis

The Blue Dasher is a small skimmer dragonfly with a powder-blue abdomen, clear wings, striped thorax, and bright reflective eyes. It is often associated with ponds, marshes, and wetlands, but adults may perch along nearby prairie edges, trail edges, and shrubby vegetation. In these photos, male Blue Dashers rest on weathered seedheads and bare twigs, offering close views of their wing posture, eye color, thorax patterning, and blue-pruinose abdomen.

For identification details and comparison with similar species, see Pachydiplax longipennis in the Field Notes section.
