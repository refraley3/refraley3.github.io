Agopostemon virescens

The Bicolored Striped Sweat Bee is a small, striking halictid with a metallic blue-green head and thorax and, in the female, a black abdomen crossed by pale bands. In these photographs, a pollen-laden female works among the violet filaments and yellow anthers of a spiderwort flower, the warm gold of the pollen contrasting with her iridescent green body.

For identification details and comparison with similar species, see Augochlorella striata in the Field Notes section.
