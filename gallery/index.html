A collection of field photography from woods, wetlands, prairies, gardens, and quiet edges of the landscape. These galleries follow birds, insects, fungi, plants, lichens, and seasonal scenes through close observation.

Animals

Birds
Movement, song, and seasonal passage shape the landscape overhead.
From reed beds and woodland edges to open water and high canopy, birds trace shifting patterns through light, weather, and migration.
Insects
Small lives woven through flowers, bark, water, and soil.
Bees, beetles, dragonflies, and other insects reveal intricate relationships between plants, seasons, and the hidden architecture of the field and forest.
Mammals
Quiet movement and familiar presence along woodland paths and neighborhood edges.
Squirrels, deer, and other mammals move through shifting light and cover, leaving subtle signs of feeding, travel, and awareness across the landscape.
Spiders
A black-and-yellow garden spider mends its orb web in morning light.
Suspended among prairie grasses, the garden spider reinforces its silk architecture, laying fresh threads across the heart of the web.
