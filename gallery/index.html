A collection of field photography from woods, wetlands, prairies, gardens, and quiet edges of the landscape. These galleries follow birds, insects, fungi, plants, lichens, and seasonal scenes through close observation.

Animals
Birds Movement, song, and seasonal passage shape the landscape overhead.
From reed beds and woodland edges to open water and high canopy, birds trace shifting patterns through light, weather, and migration.
Insects Small lives woven through flowers, bark, water, and soil.
Bees, beetles, dragonflies, and other insects reveal intricate relationships between plants, seasons, and the hidden architecture of the field and forest.
Mammals Quiet movement and familiar presence along woodland paths and neighborhood edges.
Squirrels, deer, and other mammals move through shifting light and cover, leaving subtle signs of feeding, travel, and awareness across the landscape.
Spiders A black-and-yellow garden spider mends its orb web in morning light.
Suspended among prairie grasses, the garden spider reinforces its silk architecture, laying fresh threads across the heart of the web.

Fungi & Lichens
Crust Fungi

Crust fungi are typically flat, crust-like structures that grow on various substrates.

Mushrooms Bleeding bonnet mushrooms infected with a parasitic fungus

Mushrooms are the reproductive structures of certain fungi, often visible above ground.

Rusts

Rust fungi are plant pathogens that produce rust-colored spores, often forming pustules on leaves and stems.

Lichens

Lichens are symbiotic associations between fungi and photosynthetic organisms, often forming unique and colorful growths on rocks, trees, and soil.

Plants
Ferns Native ferns add texture and shade to the landscape.
From delicate maidenhair ferns to robust ostrich ferns, these plants thrive in moist, shaded environments, providing habitat for wildlife and enhancing the natural beauty of forests and gardens.
Mosses Native mosses create a lush, green carpet on the forest floor.
These small, non-vascular plants thrive in moist, shaded environments, contributing to the biodiversity and aesthetic appeal of natural landscapes.
Wildflowers

Native wildflowers add seasonal color and support pollinators across the landscape.

Vines

Native vines add vertical interest and can cover walls, fences, and other structures.

Trees

Native trees provide shade, habitat, and seasonal interest across the landscape.

Landscapes
Landscapes

Natural landscapes across Minnesota, from woodlands and wetlands to prairies and gardens.
