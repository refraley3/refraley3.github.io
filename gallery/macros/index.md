At close range, the familiar dissolves into pattern and structure. Macros reveal the overlooked—droplets, spores, textures, and the quiet geometry of living surfaces—where scale shifts perception and small details become landscapes of their own.

## Plants

![Bur Oak](/gallery/macros/assets/plants/E22A0537-551.jpg){: .cover role="img" aria-label="Bur Oak"}

**Bur Oak**  
_Early spring bud break with emerging male catkins._  
Camera: Canon EOS 5D Mk IV  
Lens: EF100mm f/2.8L Macro IS USM  
Settings: ISO 100 • Aperture f/13 • Shutter 4 s  
E22A0537-551 • Size: 5187×3414  
{: .caption}

Tight clusters of developing catkins and velvety new leaves mark the brief moment before the flowers lengthen and release pollen into the warming air.
