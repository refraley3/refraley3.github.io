This gallery surveys the plant kingdom — from delicate wildflowers and feathery ferns to towering trees and climbing vines. Each section explores botanical structure, seasonal change, and the quiet persistence of plant life across varied habitats.

Plant Categories

Explore botanical photography organized by plant type, from prairie flowers to forest giants.

Wildflowers
Seasonal color across prairies and woodlands.
Native and naturalized blooms photographed in soft field light, from summer prairie flowers to delicate woodland ephemerals.

Trees
Pines, firs, and forest giants.
From windswept ridges to glacial sands, these trees define the character of northern and western forests and reflect quiet endurance in harsh environments.

Vines
Climbing forms linking earth and sky.
Vines trace edges between forest and field, climbing by tendrils, coils, or rootlets to find light where trees once stood.

Mosses
Soft, intricate colonies across wood and stone.
These delicate plants form lush mats in shaded and damp places, their fine fronds and sporophytes revealing beauty at the smallest scale.
