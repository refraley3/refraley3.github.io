Aquilegia canadensis

Wild columbine hangs like a small red lantern, its long spurs curving upward while the yellow stamens dangle below the flower. The red outer sepals, pale yellow inner petals, and nodding form make this spring wildflower especially graceful in close view. These photographs focus on the suspended shape of the bloom, the texture of the red spurs, and the quiet contrast between the open flower and nearby buds.

For identification details and comparison with similar species, see Aquilegia canadensis in the Field Notes section.

In each view, the flower's architecture does most of the work: five red spurs sweep upward like narrow horns, while the yellow stamens gather into a loose cluster below the open center. Tiny droplets and fine surface texture catch the light along the red sepals, giving the bloom a jeweled look without losing the airy, woodland feel of the plant.
