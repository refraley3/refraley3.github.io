Teloschistes chrysophthalmus

Golden-eye lichen forms small, shrubby tufts that stand out against bark with yellow-green, golden, and orange tones. Its curled, branching thallus and bright orange apothecia give the colony a lively, sculptural appearance, especially when low natural light catches the tuft from the side. These photographs show the species under changing light conditions and from several viewing angles, including natural-light portraits and flash-lit studies that emphasize texture, cilia, and the surrounding lichen community.

For identification details and comparison with similar species, see Teloschistes chrysophthalmus in the Field Notes section.

A compact burst of orange clings to the curve of a birch twig, its surface crowded with tiny cup-shaped apothecia that glow in low, golden light. The lichen forms a tight, tufted colony, its irregular lobes lifting just enough to create a textured, almost miniature landscape. Pale bracket fungi line the branch below, adding contrast in both color and form, while the smooth gray bark and soft background keep the focus on the vivid, sunlit cluster.
