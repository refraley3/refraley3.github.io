Field Reference Details
PlumageSharp crest, black mask, large orange bill, and long tail. Males are brilliant red. Females are a reddish-brown.
Size8" - 9" [20 -23 cm]
ColorOrange, or pink bill, and long tail. Males are brilliant red. Females are a reddish-brown.
WingsRounded,
Span 9" - 12" [25 - 31 cm]
TailNotched, Rounded, Square-tipped
HabitatNesting in open marshes, wetlands, dense brush.
Flight StyleTypically fly low to the ground. "Flap-bounding" with short bursts of wing flaps followed by brief pauses with the wings tucked near the body.
BehaviorForages while hopping on ground or in low bushes.
DietSeeds, insects, berries. Mostly vegetable. Favors sunflower seeds in feeders.

Field Reference Details
PlumageSubdued crest, black mask, red tips on the wing feathers like wax droplets.
Size6 1/2" - 8" [17 -20 cm]
ColorBrown, grey, and lemon-yellow
WingsPointed,
Span 8" - 12" [22 - 30 cm]
TailShort, Rounded, Square-tipped
HabitatOpen woodlands, fruiting trees, and orchards.
Flight StyleSteady and purposeful, they fly in tight, coordinated flocks. May hover briefly while plucking berries.
BehaviorFormation of large flocks and loose clusters of about a dozen nests. Often pass fruit between them until one eats it. Also uses fruit in mating rituals.
DietFruit and berries including juniper, dogwood, and wild cherries. Insects such as beetles, caterpillars, and ants.
