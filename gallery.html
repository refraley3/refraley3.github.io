Pine Warbler
Dendroica pinus
_21A54593273x2182
Image Details Field Reference Details
CameraCanon EOS 5D Mk IV
LensEF100-400mm f/4.5-5.6L IS USM
ISO4000
Aperturef/13.0
Shutter1/2000
PlumageOlive-green back, **two bold white wing bars**, thin pointed bill. Yellow throat and upper chest fading to whitish belly. Males brighter; females/immatures duller and grayer.
SizeLength 5–5.75" [13–15 cm]
Color Olive above; yellow throat/chest; white belly and undertail. Legs dark; bill dark and slender.
Wings Short, rounded with **two white wing bars**
Span 7.5–9.1" [19–23 cm]
Tail Medium length, slightly notched; often flicked while foraging.
Habitat Primarily pine woods and mixed conifer forests; breeds high in pines. Winters in the Southeast and along the Atlantic/Gulf coasts.
Flight Style Direct, buoyant flight with quick wingbeats; short, slightly undulating hops between treetops.
Behavior Forages methodically in mid-to-upper canopy; creeps along branches and occasionally probes pine cones. Sings from high exposed perches.
DietInsects and spiders; also seeds (especially pine) and will visit feeders for suet, peanuts, and sunflower.
